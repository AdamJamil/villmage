### The Kettle & Crow — Morning
*Sable Dunmore, Orla Fenn, Denn Corvale, Margot Thistle*

**Pell Arenway** `[8]`: *adjusts the satchel strap on my shoulder and glances toward the east pasture*

"Morning, Ham. You sleep alright? I kept thinking about that anchor pin — couldn't stop turning it over in my head."

**Aldric Moss** `[7]`: *sets a neat stack of papers on the edge of Wren's desk and straightens them unnecessarily*

"Wren. I've been by twice now about the quarterly supply order — you still haven't signed off on it, and I'd like to get the card stock settled before the end of the week."

**Sable Dunmore** `[9]`: *sets down a pot of porridge and starts wiping the counter*

Morning, all. So — did anyone make it out to that foundation yesterday, or did the whole day just swallow itself? Because I spent half of it waiting for someone to walk through that door and nobody did.

**Ham Birch** `[7]`: *tips the brim of my hat back and squints toward the east pasture*

"Well enough. Better than you, sounds like."

*pause, then quieter*

"We should get moving before the light gets too high. Denn and Orla on their way?"

**Sable Dunmore** `[9]`: *leans against the counter, gold tooth catching the light*

Day swallowed itself is right. I was here stirring a pot talking to nobody like a woman losing her mind. *glances around the room* So — did any of you actually make it out to that foundation, or did we all just lose yesterday?

**Wren Calloway** `[7]`: *looks up over the wire-rimmed glasses, pen still hovering over the leather notebook*

"I know you have, Aldric. Sit down a moment — I want to ask you something about the land records for Ham's south field before we get to your card stock."

**Pell Arenway** `[8]`: *shifts weight from one foot to the other, glancing back toward the village road*

"Haven't seen them yet. Denn's usually at the forge by now, so if he's not here he's either running late or stopped to check something on the way." 

*pats the satchel absently, feeling for the sketch in the inner pocket*

"I keep thinking about that anchor pin too. Square stock, hand-forged — that's not something you just leave behind unless you mean to come back for whatever it was holding."

**Wren Calloway** `[9]`: *taps the leather notebook with one finger, open to a page dense with small handwriting*

"Someone's been cutting a trail through Ham's south field — deliberate, stacked brush, straight line heading past the tree line. Denn Corvale told me about it yesterday. I pulled the land records last night and I want your eyes on something before I decide what I think."

**Sable Dunmore** `[8]`: *stops wiping the counter and looks up*

Well, I didn't see a soul yesterday — not here, not at the square, nowhere. So I'm guessing the walk to that foundation didn't happen yet. *tosses the rag over her shoulder* Who's still up for it? Because that iron pin isn't going anywhere, but whoever's been using that path might.

**Pell Arenway** `[7]`: *glances down the road one more time, then turns back to Ham*

"Could be Denn stopped to pull that anchor pin's measurements from memory — he was muttering about square stock dimensions the whole walk back last night." 

*pauses, tugs at the satchel strap*

"You bring water? I forgot mine on the counter like an idiot."

**Margot Thistle** `[8]`: *slides onto a stool and sets down a small cloth bundle, pushing a loose strand of hair behind her ear*

I'm up for it. But before we go anywhere — Sable, did Hargrove come in Thursday? I still haven't caught up with Wren about how the delivery went and it's nagging at me.

**Aldric Moss** `[8]`: *pulls the chair out precisely, sits down, and leans forward to look at the notebook — not touching it, but close enough to read*

"Show me what you've found. I know Ham's parcel lines better than he does — I've been keeping his boundary records since his father's time."

**Pell Arenway** `[7]`: *winces and rubs the back of my neck*

"Yeah, I know. Left it sitting right next to the kettle like it was going to walk itself out the door." 

*glances at Ham hopefully*

"Tell me you brought extra."

**Wren Calloway** `[9]`: *turns the notebook around and slides it across the desk, one finger anchoring the page*

"There. Third entry down — the original survey from Ham's father's time. That south boundary doesn't end at the tree line, Aldric. There's a quarter-acre strip past it that was never formally assigned. It just says 'common use, pending.' Pending *what*, exactly?"

**Ham Birch** `[7]`: *unslings a waterskin from my belt and holds it out without looking*

"I brought extra. Always do."

*squints down the road one more time, then settles my weight*

"Give Denn another few minutes. He'll be here."

**Margot Thistle** `[8]`: *unwraps the cloth bundle — a small jar of honey and a sprig of dried thyme, sets them on the counter absently*

No, that's what I'm trying to figure out. I never caught up with Wren after we were supposed to visit the Hargroves — she wasn't at the academy when I went back, and then everything got tangled up with that foundation business. *taps the counter once* So I don't actually know if the remedy got delivered or if it's still sitting on Wren's desk.

**Aldric Moss** `[9]`: *leans in closer, eyes narrowing at the entry, one finger tracing the boundary notation without quite touching the page*

"'Pending' means somebody died or moved before the council formalized it. I've seen this before — Ham's father had a handshake agreement with old Calloway, your grandfather, I believe, about grazing access past that line. Nothing was ever recorded because nobody thought it needed to be."

*sits back, folds arms*

"Which means whoever's cutting that trail might think it's open land. And technically, they're not entirely wrong."

**Pell Arenway** `[7]`: *takes the waterskin gratefully, drinks, and hands it back*

"Thanks, Ham. You're a better neighbor than I deserve."

*settles the satchel and glances toward the east pasture, voice dropping a notch*

"I keep looking at that sketch. The path, the foundation, the anchor pin — it all points at someone local. Someone who knew that stone pile was out there before any of us did."

**Sable Dunmore** `[8]`: *takes the honey jar and thyme sprig, turning them over in her hands before setting them back down*

That remedy's been sitting somewhere for two days now, and that girl's still coughing. *looks at Margot* We need to sort that out today — either find Wren or just take it to the Hargroves ourselves. *glances at Orla and Denn* And then we finish that walk to the foundation, because I've been thinking about what Orla said about that dull blade and I haven't stopped.

**Wren Calloway** `[8]`: *takes the notebook back and studies the entry again, one fingernail tapping the word 'pending'*

"That's what I thought. Now here's what I want to know — Denn said Orla was with him and Ham when they found that trail. And somebody went to real trouble cutting it, Aldric. Stacked brush, straight line. That's not a wanderer. That's someone who thinks they know where they're going."

**Ham Birch** `[7]`: *takes the waterskin back and loops it on my belt, then looks at Pell steadily*

"Could be local. Could be someone who *was* local."

*settles my hat and looks toward the east pasture*

"Let's not get ahead of what we can see with our own eyes. We go back to that foundation, we look at what's there, and we let the stone tell us what it tells us."

**Pell Arenway** `[7]`: *nods slowly, letting Ham's words settle*

"You're right. Stone first, theories after."

*adjusts the satchel and pulls the sketch from the inner pocket, unfolding it carefully*

"I marked the dead elm and the creek crossing last night. If Denn doesn't show in five minutes, we should start walking and let him catch up — he knows the way."

**Wren Calloway** `[8]`: *removes glasses and cleans them slowly on a handkerchief, thinking*

"So the question is whether Orla was surprised when they found that trail, or whether she was the one who led them to it."

**Margot Thistle** `[8]`: *picks up the honey jar and tucks it back into the bundle*

Right. The remedy's with Wren — I left it at the academy Day 2 morning, dosing slip and all. But she wasn't there when I went back that afternoon, desk was locked up. So either she made the visit without me or it's still sitting there. *looks at Sable* I say we stop by the academy first, sort the Hargrove business, and then head for that foundation. I don't want another day where we talk about doing three things and do none of them.

**Wren Calloway** `[8]`: *puts the glasses back on and looks at Aldric steadily*

"I'm not accusing anyone. But Orla knows land better than most people in this village, and she doesn't stumble onto things by accident."

**Pell Arenway** `[4]`: *folds the sketch back up and tucks it away, then looks down the road one last time*

"Alright, five minutes is five minutes. Let's walk — Denn'll see our tracks through the east pasture easy enough."

*starts walking, then glances sideways at Ham*

"You notice anything different with your goats this morning? They were all bunched up at the south end of the pen when I passed your gate."

**Sable Dunmore** `[7]`: *nods at Margot, then looks at Denn and Orla*

That's the plan then. Academy first, sort the remedy, then we walk. *pauses, tapping the counter* Denn, Orla — you two still good for the foundation? Because I want to see that iron pin myself before I spend another night thinking about it.

**Ham Birch** `[7]`: *walks alongside Pell, matching his pace but not hurrying*

"They were bunched up. Noticed it too."

*quiet for a few steps*

"Could be nothing. Could be they smelled something down by the south fence again. I'll check when we're back."

**Aldric Moss** `[8]`: *unfolds arms and places both hands flat on the desk, thinking carefully*

"You're right that Orla doesn't stumble. But I'll say this — if she's the one who cut that trail, she'd have told you herself by now. Orla doesn't do things sideways."

*pauses, then quieter*

"Who else has been asking about unassigned parcels lately? Because I keep the land records too, Wren, and somebody pulled the boundary index two weeks ago and didn't sign the log."

**Denn Corvale** `[7]`: *wraps both hands around his mug and nods once*

I'm good for it. But that iron pin — I want a closer look before anyone starts kicking around the foundation. If it's what I think it is, it'll tell us more than the path does.

**Aldric Moss** `[9]`: *straightens slightly, voice dropping*

"I keep that log for a reason, Wren. Somebody pulled the boundary index and didn't sign out. That's not forgetting — that's choosing not to leave a mark."

*meets Wren's eyes*

"You want me to check the dates against who had the key?"

**Pell Arenway** `[4]`: *matches Ham's pace, quiet for a moment, then nods*

"Yeah. Could be the same something that's been using that path."

*kicks a clod of dirt absently as we walk*

"Let's just get to the foundation. I want to see if that anchor pin's still sitting where Denn found it."

**Margot Thistle** `[7]`: *nods at Denn, then glances at Sable*

Good. Academy first — if Wren's there, we sort it in five minutes. If she's not, I'm taking the remedy myself and we go straight to the Hargroves. That girl's been coughing for almost two weeks now and I'm done waiting on locked desks.

**Ham Birch** `[3]`: *walks steadily beside Pell, eyes on the ground ahead, reading the grass*

"We'll see."

**Wren Calloway** `[9]`: *nods slowly, holding Aldric's gaze*

"Do that. And don't tell anyone you're checking — not even Ham. I want to know who pulled that index before whoever it is knows we're looking."

**Margot Thistle** `[7]`: *stands up from the stool and tucks the cloth bundle under her arm*

Right. Let's go then, before we talk ourselves into another lost day. *glances at Orla* You coming?

**Pell Arenway** `[4]`: *nods, matching Ham's quiet pace, then after a beat*

"Yeah."

*walks in silence for a few steps, eyes scanning the grass line where the east pasture starts to dip*

"There's the dead elm."

**Aldric Moss** `[8]`: *nods once, firmly, and stands — pushing the chair back into place*

"I'll check the key log tonight after I close. And Wren — while I'm being useful, sign the supply order. The card stock price goes up Friday."

**Sable Dunmore** `[7]`: *grabs a clean rag and starts wiping her hands, already moving toward the door*

Orla's been quiet all morning and I'm not rushing her, but yes — let's move before this turns into Day 2 again. *looks at Orla with a small nod* We'll wait if you need a minute.

**Wren Calloway** `[7]`: *picks up the supply order from the stack, uncaps the pen, and signs it with a quick, precise hand*

"There. Now go check that key log and leave my card stock alone."

**Pell Arenway** `[7]`: *spots the dead elm's pale trunk against the tree line and feels a small knot of anticipation tighten*

"Still standing, still dead. Creek crossing should be just past that low spot — you remember if we came at it from the left or right of that big root ball?"

**Orla Fenn** `[4]`: *stands up from the corner table, pulling on her jacket*

I'm coming. *pauses, looks at Denn* Bring something to pry with. If that pin's anchored into the foundation stone, we'll want to see what's underneath without breaking it.

**Pell Arenway** `[7]`: *squints at the root ball, then points just to the right of it*

"Right side. I remember because I tripped on that exposed root and you didn't say a word about it, which I appreciated."

**Wren Calloway** `[3]`: *watches Aldric go, then pulls the leather notebook back and uncaps the pen*

*writes for a moment — date, Aldric's name, the unsigned log entry, the key detail — then pauses, pen hovering*

*mutters to no one*

"Pending."

*closes the notebook and tucks it under one arm, then begins straightening the desk for the morning's lessons*

**Sable Dunmore** `[7]`: *grabs a thin pry bar from behind the counter and tosses it to Denn*

Keep that. I've been using it to open crates, but it'll do the job. *pulls the door open and holds it* Academy's a five-minute walk if we stop talking now.

**Ham Birch** `[3]`: *walks steadily past the root ball on the right side, eyes scanning the ground*

"Didn't say a word then, won't say a word now."

*pauses at the creek crossing, checking the far bank*

"Water's lower than yesterday. Someone else been through here — look at that mud."

**Wren Calloway** `[3]`: *finishes straightening the desk, then pauses — looks at the empty schoolroom chairs, the three wobbly bookshelves, the morning light coming through the windows*

*opens the leather notebook again, flips past the land record notes to a fresh page, and writes the date*

*mutters while writing*

"Three days and I've had more visitors about boundary disputes than lessons. If the children don't come back after harvest, I'll have to rename this place Calloway's Land Office."

*closes the notebook, tucks it under one arm, and begins setting out the day's reading primers on the front desks — just in case*

**Denn Corvale** `[7]`: *catches the pry bar one-handed and tucks it through his belt loop, then grabs his mug and drains the last of it*

Good. *sets the mug down on the counter and heads for the door* And Sable — if Wren's not there, ask Margot about the land records too. I asked Wren about what's past that south tree line on Day 2 and never got an answer.

**Pell Arenway** `[8]`: *crouches at the creek bank, studying the mud, then looks up at Ham*

"That's fresh. Boot print, not hoof. Someone crossed here since last night's dew settled."

*stands slowly, eyes following the far bank toward where the foundation should be*

"We're not the only ones checking on that anchor pin this morning."

**Wren Calloway** `[3]`: *sets the last reading primer down on the front desk, then stands still for a moment, looking at the empty chairs*

*speaks quietly to the room*

"Three days running."

*adjusts the primer so its edge lines up with the desk corner, then walks to the window and looks out at the village road, notebook tucked under one arm*

**Pell Arenway** `[8]`: *straightens up from the creek bank, eyes still on the boot print, voice low*

"That's a working boot, not a walking boot. Whoever that is came here with a purpose."

*glances at Ham, then back toward the foundation site*

"We should move quiet."

**Sable Dunmore** `[7]`: *holds the door open and nods at Denn as he passes*

Land records — good thought. If that foundation's on common land, somebody owned it before it was common. *glances back at Margot* You know where Wren keeps the parish surveys, or is that locked up too?

**Wren Calloway** `[3]`: *still looking out the window at the empty road, speaks without turning around*

"Three days and not a single child. Harvest doesn't last forever, but habits do."

*turns back to the room, sets the notebook on the desk, and adjusts one of the reading primers a quarter inch to the left*

**Pell Arenway** `[7]`: *nods, matching Ham's pace, voice dropped low*

"Quiet it is."

*steps carefully across the creek, eyes tracking the boot print's direction, one hand steadying the satchel so nothing shifts or clinks*

**Margot Thistle** `[8]`: *glances back at Sable as she steps through the door*

Parish surveys are in the back cabinet — Wren showed me once when I was looking up property lines for the shop lease. If it's locked, I know which drawer she keeps the key in. *adjusts the bundle under her arm* Let's just hope she's actually there this time.

**Aldric Moss** `[3]`: *pauses at the door, hand on the frame, and looks back at Wren standing alone in the empty schoolroom*

"Wren. For what it's worth — when the children do come back, they'll be lucky you kept setting out the primers."

*adjusts the measuring tape around his neck, then quieter*

"I'll have the key log checked by tomorrow morning."
