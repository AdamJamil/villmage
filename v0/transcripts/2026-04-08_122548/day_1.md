### The Kettle & Crow — Morning
*Sable Dunmore, Pell Arenway, Ham Birch, Orla Fenn, Denn Corvale*

**Sable Dunmore** `[9]`: *wipes down the bar with a rag, glancing around at the morning crowd*

"Well, look at this — half the village in my chairs before I've even got the bread out. Give me ten minutes and there'll be coffee. Pell, you look like you didn't sleep. Who kept you up?"

**Pell Arenway** `[9]`: *shifts on the stool, satchel sliding off one shoulder and spilling a folded note onto the floor*

"Nobody kept me up — I kept *myself* up. Found an old survey map stuffed behind my pantry shelf and couldn't stop comparing it to my own notes. Half the footpaths south of the creek aren't even on it, Sable. It's criminal."

*picks up the fallen note, squints at it, then tucks it back in the satchel*

"Coffee would save my life, though. Genuinely."

**Sable Dunmore** `[9]`: *sets a kettle on the stove and gives Pell a look that's half sympathy, half amusement*

"Footpaths not on a map. The scandal. Somebody alert the mayor."

*glances over at the others*

"Ham, you're quiet this morning — that's either good news or you're about to tell me your apple prices went up again."

**Sable Dunmore** `[8]`: *turns back to the kettle as it starts to hiss, pulling down mugs with practiced ease*

"And Orla — I see you back there trying to be invisible. It's not working. You want the usual or are you feeling adventurous today?"

**Sable Dunmore** `[7]`: *slides the first mug of coffee across to Pell, then leans on the bar*

"Denn, I know you're over there pretending to read something. You want eggs or are you just here for the atmosphere?"

**Pell Arenway** `[7]`: *wraps both hands around the mug like it's the only warm thing left in the world, takes a long sip*

"Sable, this is perfect. You've saved a life today and it's not even eight o'clock."

*glances over at Ham with a grin*

"How are the goats, Ham? Clover still chewing through fence posts?"

**Sable Dunmore** `[8]`: *slides a second mug down the bar toward Denn's end without waiting for an answer, then starts cracking eggs into a bowl*

"Pell, you keep calling it 'saving your life' and one of these days I'm going to start charging you accordingly."

*flashes the gold tooth in a grin*

"Now somebody tell me something interesting before I have to go back there and actually work."

**Sable Dunmore** `[8]`: *cracks another egg one-handed, flicking the shell into a bucket without looking*

"Come on, nothing? Nobody's got a neighborly dispute, a strange noise in the night, a chicken gone missing? I'm dying up here."

**Pell Arenway** `[8]`: *sets down the mug and leans forward conspiratorially*

"Actually — I was cutting through the meadow path yesterday, the one past the old Harwick fence? There's a tree down across it. Big one. Looked like it'd been down a while, but someone's dragged branches off to the side like they were clearing a way through."

*shrugs*

"Might be nothing. Might be someone's got a shortcut they don't want the rest of us knowing about."

**Sable Dunmore** `[9]`: *pauses mid-crack on an egg, eyebrow going up*

"Past the Harwick fence? That path hasn't been used regular since old Harwick moved off. Somebody clearing branches means somebody's using it — and anybody who *wanted* to use it wouldn't need to be sneaky about it."

*finishes cracking the egg, wipes her hands on her apron, and looks at Ham and Orla*

"Either of you been out that way lately?"

**Sable Dunmore** `[8]`: *pours beaten eggs into the hot pan, letting them sizzle while keeping her eyes on the room*

"Quiet bunch today. That's a yes, a no, or a 'mind your business, Sable' — and we all know which one of those I'm worst at."

**Sable Dunmore** `[8]`: *slides a plate of eggs toward Denn's end of the bar whether he asked or not, then turns back to Pell*

"You know what, I'm going to walk out that way myself later. Curiosity's a disease and I've never once tried to cure it."

*looks at Orla again, softening her voice just a touch*

"Orla, honey, your usual's coming. You don't have to ask."

**Ham Birch** `[7]`: *shifts on the stool, wraps a thick hand around the coffee mug Sable's slid his way*

"Clover's fine. She chews what she chews. Not much I can do about it that I haven't tried."

*takes a slow sip, then sets the mug down*

"Haven't been past the Harwick fence in weeks. No reason to. But if somebody's clearing a path out there, it's not for a stroll — that way only leads to the creek or the back of my south field."

**Pell Arenway** `[8]`: *sets the mug down and looks at Ham, eyebrows lifting*

"Your south field? Huh. That's... specific. I figured it was just someone dodging mud on the main trail, but if there's nothing else out that way..."

*taps the side of the satchel absently, already thinking about routes*

"I might swing back through there on my afternoon loop. I've got a delivery out to the Creel place anyway — wouldn't be much of a detour."

**Sable Dunmore** `[8]`: *leans on the bar, pointing a spatula loosely between Pell and Ham*

"Well, if it leads to Ham's south field, then either somebody's helping themselves to something or they're lost in a very specific way. Pell, you go look — but take the main road to Creel's first, don't go getting yourself turned around chasing mysteries on no sleep."

**Ham Birch** `[7]`: *takes another slow sip of coffee, then sets the mug down with a quiet thud*

"If somebody's been in my south field, I'd have noticed. Nothing's been touched that I can see."

*pauses, thumbing the rim of the mug*

"Still. I'll walk the fence line this afternoon."

**Sable Dunmore** `[7]`: *nods at Ham, then turns back to the eggs on the stove, flipping them with a practiced flick*

"Good. You two do your respective poking around and report back to me tonight — I'll have the lamb stew on and you can tell me it was a deer trail and I got worked up over nothing."

**Sable Dunmore** `[6]`: *slides Orla's usual across the bar — dark coffee, no sugar, with a warm roll on the side — and gives her a quick wink*

"Alright, lamb stew tonight, mystery patrol this afternoon. Denn, eat your eggs before they go cold — I didn't make them for decoration."

**Sable Dunmore** `[7]`: *watches Orla take the roll and coffee without a word, then turns back to wipe down the counter, satisfied*

"Quietest morning I've had in weeks and somehow we've still managed to organize a whole patrol. That's the power of coffee and eggs, I suppose."

**Orla Fenn** `[6]`: *tears a small piece off the roll, chews it slowly, then speaks without looking up*

"I was out past Harwick's last week. Needed a straight piece of ash for a chair back."

*pauses, takes a sip of coffee*

"Didn't clear any path, though."

### Fenwick Meadow — Afternoon
*Ham Birch, Pell Arenway*

**Pell Arenway** `[7]`: "Hey, Ham! Heading out to check that fence line? I'm about to loop past the Harwick path on my way to the Creels' — figured I'd see if that cleared section lines up with anything near your south field."

**Sable Dunmore** `[8]`: *wipes down the bar with a rag, glancing up at the two of them*

"Well now, afternoon already and I've still got lamb to braise. Wren, Margot — either of you want something, or are you just here to keep my chairs warm?"

**Aldric Moss** `[4]`: *adjusts the measuring tape around his neck and glances around the Academy with a slight frown, as though calculating why he's here instead of behind his counter*

"Denn. Didn't expect to find you here in the middle of a workday."

**Ham Birch** `[7]`: *adjusts hat and squints toward the south field*

"Yeah, heading down now. If you're passing that way, have a look whether the clearing goes any further past where you saw it this morning. I'll start at the fence and work back."

**Wren Calloway** `[7]`: *looks up from the leather notebook, pen still poised mid-sentence, peering over the rim of her glasses*

"I'll take a cup of whatever's already hot, Sable. And I'm not keeping your chairs warm — I'm keeping them from getting lonely. Nobody else seems interested in sitting down anywhere today."

**Denn Corvale** `[6]`: *sets down a heavy leather satchel near the door and rolls one shoulder with a quiet grunt*

"Could say the same to you, Aldric. I'm dropping off a hinge pin for that display case Headmaster Wren's been after me about for three weeks."

**Pell Arenway** `[7]`: *shifts the satchel strap on my shoulder and nods*

"Will do. I've got a drop at the Creels' anyway, so it's no trouble. If I find anything worth mentioning, I'll catch you at Sable's tonight — she's doing that lamb stew, remember."

**Aldric Moss** `[6]`: *runs a thumb along the edge of the measuring tape, eyes flicking to the satchel*

"Three weeks. That's about right for you." *a beat, then quieter, almost conversational* "While you're here — I've got your ledger ready back at the store. You're overdue on reconciling April."

**Sable Dunmore** `[7]`: *sets a mug of cider in front of Wren, still warm from the pot near the hearth*

"Lonely chairs. That's poetic enough to go in one of your books." *turns to Margot* "And you, Margot? You eating, drinking, or just eavesdropping? All three are welcome, but only two come with a bill."

**Pell Arenway** `[3]`: *grins and pats the satchel*

"Lamb stew's the only deadline I never miss. Alright, I'm off — Creels won't deliver themselves."

*gives a half-wave and starts down the meadow path toward Harwick, already lengthening my stride*

**Aldric Moss** `[7]`: *the corner of his mouth twitches — not quite a smile, more an acknowledgment that the jab landed where he aimed it*

"Hinge pin. Right. Well, while you're delivering metalwork to Wren, you might remind him I'm still waiting on his signature for the quarterly supply order. Man'll debate the placement of a semicolon for an hour but can't sign his name on a dotted line."

**Ham Birch** `[3]`: *watches Pell's figure shrink down the path, then turns slowly toward the south field, settling the hat lower against the afternoon glare*

"Right then."

*starts walking, boot finding the familiar rut in the meadow grass without looking down*

**Aldric Moss** `[6]`: *glances toward the hallway leading to Wren's office, then back at Denn*

"I'll mention it myself if he's in. Came to drop off the updated inventory sheets — he asked for them last Tuesday, so naturally I had them ready by Wednesday."

**Pell Arenway** `[2]`: *already a dozen strides down the path, I pause and half-turn back, calling over my shoulder*

"Ham — if your goats get through that fence before you fix it, I'm not chasing them this time!"

*grins to myself and keeps walking, knowing full well I absolutely would*

**Margot Thistle** `[7]`: *leans back in the chair, one hand absently tucking a loose strand of hair behind her ear*

"All three, probably, but I'll start with a cider like Wren's. And for the record, Sable, your eavesdropping is free but mine is *invaluable* — I'm the only one in this village who'll tell you your lamb needs more rosemary before you serve it, not after."

**Denn Corvale** `[6]`: *exhales through his nose — not quite a laugh, but close*

"Wednesday. Course you did."

*picks up the satchel again and shifts it to the other hand*

"I'll get to the ledger when I get to it, Aldric. April's not going anywhere."

**Ham Birch** `[4]`: *lets out a short breath through the nose, half-laugh, half-sigh, and calls back without turning around*

"They're *my* goats, Pell. They just don't know it yet."

*shakes head once and keeps walking, the tall meadow grass brushing against those perpetually muddy boots*

"More rosemary — she says this to me, in my own kitchen. Margot, the day I need your rosemary advice is the day I hang up my apron and let Aldric take over the cooking." *pauses, considering* "Actually, don't tell him I said that. He'd show up with a menu."

"April goes somewhere eventually, Denn. It goes into May. And then I'm reconciling two months instead of one."

*smooths the measuring tape against his chest and turns toward Wren's office himself, muttering just loud enough to be heard*

"Nobody rushes in this village except me."

**Pell Arenway** `[2]`: *already well down the path, I hear Ham's reply and let out a proper laugh — the kind that carries across open meadow*

*shakes head, still grinning, and picks up the pace toward the Harwick fork, fingers already checking the satchel flap out of habit*

**Margot Thistle** `[7]`: *wraps both hands around the warm mug and takes a sip, eyebrows lifting approvingly*

"Your cider's better than your threats, Sable. And Aldric with a menu — honestly, I'd pay to see that disaster unfold." *glances sideways at Wren's notebook* "What are you scribbling in there, anyway? You've been at it since I walked in."

**Denn Corvale** `[4]`: *stops in the hallway and half-turns back, voice unhurried*

"You're not wrong, Aldric. But a hinge pin that don't fit right'll cause more trouble than a late ledger."

*shifts the satchel and continues toward Wren's office*

"I'll tell him about your supply order."

**Ham Birch** `[2]`: *walks in silence for a while, the meadow opening up toward the south field, the fence line coming into view against the tree line*

*slows, eyes tracing the posts from left to right, looking for anything leaning or gaps where there shouldn't be*

"Alright. Let's see what we've got."

**Wren Calloway** `[8]`: *glances down at the notebook, then back up at Margot over the rim of her glasses*

"Notes. On everything and nothing." *takes a slow sip of cider* "Not a single student showed up this morning, so I've had plenty of time to fill pages."

**Aldric Moss** `[3]`: *watches Denn's back disappear down the hallway, then stands still for a moment, straightening the inventory sheets against his thigh*

"A late ledger *is* trouble, Denn. You just can't hear it squeaking."

*follows at a measured distance, not hurrying, because he never hurries — he's simply always already on time*

**Pell Arenway** `[3]`: *already rounding the bend toward the Harwick fork, I glance back one last time — Ham's just a dark shape against the fence line now*

*turns forward, adjusts the satchel, and settles into my walking rhythm, the path narrowing where the meadow grass gives way to packed dirt and old tree roots*

"Creel place first, then the path. Then stew."

**Denn Corvale** `[4]`: *pauses in the hallway, not turning around this time, just letting the words land*

"That's the difference between us, Aldric. I listen for the squeak *before* it starts."

**Wren Calloway** `[7]`: *closes the notebook and sets the pen down with a quiet click*

"Three families pulled their children out this term. Said they'd learn more helping with the harvest." *pauses, runs a thumb along the notebook's spine* "They're not wrong about the harvest part. They're wrong about everything else."

**Aldric Moss** `[4]`: *stands in the hallway for a moment, watching the space where Denn was, then shakes his head once — barely perceptible*

"You don't listen for anything, Denn. You just work slow and call it philosophy."

*smooths the inventory sheets one final time and follows, keeping his measured distance*

**Margot Thistle** `[8]`: *sets the mug down, expression shifting from amused to something more careful*

"Three families." *quiet for a moment* "Which three?"

"I'll wait out here. No sense crowding Wren's office. He gets twitchy with more than one visitor."

**Margot Thistle** `[8]`: *watches Wren's face carefully, thumb tracing the rim of the mug*

"Because if one of them's the Hargroves, their youngest has been coming to my shop with a cough that won't quit. Kid needs looking after, not hauling grain."

**Pell Arenway** `[2]`: *walking alone now, the path quiet except for birdsong and my own footsteps*

*glances down at the satchel, then at the tree line to the left where the Harwick path should fork off in another quarter mile*

*pats my coat pocket to make sure the little charcoal pencil is still there — the one I keep for sketching trail notes on the backs of delivery receipts*

**Aldric Moss** `[3]`: *leans against the wall, listening to the silence of the hallway, then adjusts the inventory sheets so their edges are perfectly aligned*

*says nothing for a moment, because there's nobody to say it to, and that's fine*

**Wren Calloway** `[8]`: *meets Margot's eyes, then looks down at the notebook, fingers resting on its cover*

"The Hargroves, the Fenns, and Corrigan's boy." *pause* "And yes, the Hargrove girl was coughing last week in class. I noticed."

"Afternoon! Creels! Got a delivery!"

**Aldric Moss** `[3]`: *stands in the quiet hallway, fingers drumming once against the inventory sheets, then stopping — because drumming is a fidget, and fidgets are for people who don't plan their time properly*

*checks the alignment of the inventory sheets one more time, then folds his arms loosely, the measuring tape swaying slightly*

"Wren better not keep him in there debating hinge metallurgy. I've got a store to get back to."

*says it to no one, which is becoming a habit today*

"On the step, Creels."
