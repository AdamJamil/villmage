**Margot Thistle** `[9]`: *sets a small cloth-wrapped bundle on the nearest desk, tucking a loose strand of hair back behind the chopstick*

"Both, but mostly the first. Elecampane, thyme, honey base — enough for a week. You have those symptom notes started yet?"

**Margot Thistle** `[7]`: *taps the bundle lightly with two fingers*

"That's the blend. Dosing's on the slip inside — twice daily, steamed not boiled, or it'll go bitter and she won't take it. Now — symptom notes. Where are we?"

**Pell Arenway** `[7]`: "Right, sorry about that — I got turned around at the fork and just followed my feet here out of habit." I hitch the satchel strap higher on my shoulder and pull out the folded delivery receipt with yesterday's sketch. "I've got the path marked out from yesterday. Should we head to your fence line from here, or circle back to the gate first?"

**Margot Thistle** `[8]`: *leans a hip against the desk, arms crossed*

"Sorry — I'm getting ahead of myself. But yes, the blend's done. What I need from you is a dated page: when the cough started, wet or dry, worse at night or morning, any fever. Anything the mother's mentioned. I want it written down before we hand this off, not after."

**Ham Birch** `[8]`: "From here's fine. No sense doubling back." I squint toward the tree line past the south field and start walking. "Denn, you brought something to check the cut marks?"

**Wren Calloway** `[8]`: *pulls the worn leather notebook from her cardigan pocket and flips it open to a marked page, setting it on the desk beside the bundle*

"I started it last night. Cough began nine days ago — dry at first, wet by day four. Mother says worse after sundown. No fever she's noticed, but the girl's been pale and off her food."

*taps the page with one finger*

"That's what I have. If you want more, you'll need to see her yourself."

**Denn Corvale** `[8]`: I pat the heavy leather tool roll at my side. "Got a hand chisel and a drawknife. Should be able to tell you if those cuts were axe, saw, or something else." I fall into step beside Ham without another word.

**Margot Thistle** `[7]`: *picks up the notebook, scans the page slowly, then sets it back down with a small nod*

"Nine days is longer than I'd like. This is good — thank you." 

*pauses, looks at Wren directly*

"I want to see her. Can you get me into that house without making it feel like a visit from the authorities?"

**Ham Birch** `[3]`: I nod at Denn without looking at him — that's all that needs saying on that front. "The scrape's about two hundred paces past the big ash stump. You'll see it." I walk steady, not fast, eyes already reading the fence line ahead.

**Wren Calloway** `[8]`: *adjusts glasses, considers for a moment*

"I'll walk you over myself after lessons. The mother trusts me — she'll take it better as me checking on schoolwork the girl's missing, and you just happen to be along." 

*pauses, fixes Margot with a look* 

"But you let me do the talking at the door."

**Pell Arenway** `[4]`: I quicken my pace to keep up with Ham's steady walk, glancing back to make sure Orla's coming too. "Orla, you said last week the brush out by Harwick looked untouched — once Denn checks the fence scrape, might be worth having you look at those stacked branches too, see if whoever cut them used the same tool."

**Margot Thistle** `[7]`: *holds up both hands, palms out*

"Wouldn't dream of it. You talk, I'll carry the bundle and look harmless."

**Orla Fenn** `[7]`: I give Pell a short nod. "I saw the brush. I'll look." I shift the hatchet on my belt and keep walking.

**Margot Thistle** `[3]`: *smirks, then glances down at the bundle on the desk*

"For the record, I always look harmless. It's the apron that does it — people see stains and pockets and assume 'helpful.'"

*straightens up, tone shifting slightly*

"After lessons, then. I'll be at the shop until you come by."

**Pell Arenway** `[3]`: I fall into stride beside Denn, stealing a glance at his tool roll. "You know, if those cuts match an axe rather than a saw, that narrows it down quite a bit — not many people around here bother hauling an axe off the main road." I pat my satchel where the sketch is tucked. "I marked where the stacking started yesterday. We'll know soon enough."

**Wren Calloway** `[4]`: *allows a brief, dry smile*

"After lessons. And Margot — if you're going to look harmless, leave the chopstick. It makes you look like you're about to perform surgery."

*picks up the notebook and tucks it back into her cardigan pocket, then turns back toward the reading shelf*

"Now go on. I've got twelve children arriving in twenty minutes and half of them still can't spell 'February.'"

**Ham Birch** `[3]`: I keep my eyes on the fence line as we walk, counting posts out of habit. "Talk less, look more. We're close."

**Wren Calloway** `[2]`: *watches Margot head for the door, then calls after her without turning around*

"The chopstick stays."

*begins straightening the primers on the front desk, muttering to herself about February and the absurdity of the English language*

**Pell Arenway** `[2]`: I shut my mouth and nod, eyes dropping to the fence line. After a few paces I spot it — a pale gouge low on the rail, fresh wood showing through. I point without a word and look at Denn.

**Margot Thistle** `[2]`: *pauses at the door, one hand on the frame, and touches the chopstick with the other*

"The chopstick *always* stays."

*grins, tucks the bundle under her arm — wait, no, the bundle stays on the desk for Wren to deliver — sets it back down, pats it once*

"Bundle's yours to hold until this afternoon. I'll be at the shop."

*heads out into the morning air*

*Margot Thistle left.*

**Denn Corvale** `[9]`: I kneel beside the rail, unrolling the tool leather on the ground. I run my thumb along the gouge first — feeling the grain, the direction, the depth — then hold the chisel edge up against it for comparison. "Hatchet, not an axe. Short stroke, angled down." I look up at Orla's belt. "Like yours, but duller."

**Orla Fenn** `[8]`: I look down at the hatchet on my belt, then back at the gouge. My jaw tightens, but I unhook the hatchet and hold it out to Denn, handle first. "Then check mine against it. Rule it out."
