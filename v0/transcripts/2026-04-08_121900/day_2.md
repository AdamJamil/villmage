### The Kettle & Crow — Morning
*Pell Arenway, Orla Fenn, Sable Dunmore, Ham Birch*

**Pell Arenway** `[8]`: *sets satchel on the table with a thud that's louder than intended, a loose button rolling out across the wood*

Morning, everyone. Sable, whatever you've got brewing today, I need it — I've got half the village to cross before noon and I'm already behind.

**Sable Dunmore** `[8]`: *already sliding a mug across the counter before Pell's even finished talking, the coffee dark enough to stand a spoon in*

Half the village before noon — so a Tuesday for you, then. *flashes the gold tooth* Sit, drink that, and tell me whose business you're minding first so I know what I'm hearing about later.

**Pell Arenway** `[8]`: *catches the loose button before it rolls off the table edge, pockets it with a sheepish grin*

First stop's the Fenwick place, then swinging past the Draycott farm on the way back through. Routine stuff. *takes a long sip of the coffee and winces appreciatively* Oh, that's mean. That's perfect. — Ham, how's Duchess?

**Pell Arenway** `[8]`: *grins over the rim of the mug*

Duchess is fine, Ham. She's better than fine. Last time I passed your fence she followed me the whole length of it like I was leading a parade. I think she's planning to elope with me.

**Pell Arenway** `[8]`: *laughs and realizes he just answered his own question before Ham could*

Sorry, Ham — didn't mean to steal your line. But honestly, that goat has opinions about me and I think you should respect her choices.

**Sable Dunmore** `[8]`: *wipes down the counter with a rag, shaking her head at Pell talking to himself*

Pell, you just had a full conversation with yourself about another man's goat. I want you to sit with that. *glances at Ham* Ham, you're allowed to defend your goat's honor anytime now.

**Ham Birch** `[8]`: *takes a slow sip of his own coffee, sets it down, looks at Pell with the patience of a man who's had this conversation before*

Duchess doesn't love you, Pell. She's studying you. There's a difference, and when she figures you out, you'll know it.

**Pell Arenway** `[8]`: *leans back, pointing at Ham with the coffee mug*

Studying me. Right. That's what she's doing when she presses her whole face through the fence slats and bleats until I scratch her ears. Very scientific of her. *takes another sip, then shifts tone* Speaking of your end of things, Ham — you haven't had any strangers come by the farm lately, have you? Someone selling soap, maybe? Brown paper, little sprig on top?

**Sable Dunmore** `[8]`: *stops wiping the counter, rag going still*

Soap with a sprig on top? That's specific, Pell. *leans forward on her elbows* Who's selling fancy soap door-to-door in a village this size, and more importantly, why haven't they come to me?

**Sable Dunmore** `[9]`: *straightens up, pointing the rag at Pell*

Because I will take it personally if someone's running a cottage industry in this village and hasn't had the decency to try selling to the woman who feeds everyone. *glances at Ham* You seen anything like that?

**Sable Dunmore** `[8]`: *narrows her eyes at Pell, then at Ham, then back at Pell*

Because I've had three people this week tell me the village feels quieter than it used to, and now someone's going door-to-door selling pretty soap? That's either someone new passing through or someone here with a side project, and either way I want to know about it.

**Sable Dunmore** `[8]`: *taps the rag against the counter once, then tosses it over her shoulder*

Orla, you've been sitting there quiet as a church mouse through all this. You seen anyone new around the east side lately?

**Pell Arenway** `[8]`: *glances at Sable, then back down at his coffee, choosing his words carefully*

I'm not trying to make a thing of it — I just noticed the same wrapping at a couple of stops yesterday. Brown paper, little herb sprig. Could be nothing, could be someone from Bretten making a circuit. *looks at Ham* That's why I'm asking — figured if they'd come your way, you'd have noticed.

**Sable Dunmore** `[7]`: *leans both elbows on the counter, eyes moving between Pell and Ham*

Bretten's a long way to come for soap money. *drums her fingers once* And whoever it is knows how to make a thing look pretty, which means they're not just selling soap — they're selling an idea. That's more interesting to me than the product.

**Pell Arenway** `[7]`: *nods slowly at Sable, turning the mug in his hands*

That's exactly what caught my eye — it's not just soap, it's *presented*. Someone's thought about it. *glances at Orla* You get any deliveries lately that didn't come through me? No judgment, I just like to know if I've got competition.

**Sable Dunmore** `[7]`: *eyes settling on Orla with genuine curiosity but no pressure, just a warm lean across the counter*

Don't everybody look at her at once. *softens her voice* But really, Orla — anything odd come past your way lately? Doesn't have to be soap.

**Ham Birch** `[6]`: *sets down his mug, thinks for a moment*

Nobody's been by the farm selling anything. But I'll say this — Duchess got into the herb garden yesterday and knocked over a whole row of lavender starts, so if someone's making soap with sprigs on it, I'd appreciate them coming to buy their ingredients proper instead of letting my goat do their shopping for them.

**Sable Dunmore** `[7]`: *lets out a laugh that's louder than she meant, slapping the counter once*

Ham, if Duchess is out there freelancing as an herb supplier, I'm putting her on the payroll before whoever's making that soap does. *turns back to Pell, still grinning but with something thoughtful behind it* Lavender sprigs, though — that narrows it down. Somebody local's growing it, or somebody local's losing it to a goat.

**Sable Dunmore** `[7]`: *still chuckling, but the gears are turning*

Alright, so we've got mystery soap, lavender sprigs, and Duchess running an unlicensed herb operation. *looks at Orla again, gentler* No pressure, love — but you're the quietest person at a table full of people who won't shut up, and that usually means you know something.

**Sable Dunmore** `[7]`: *leans back from the counter, crossing her arms, letting the silence do a little work*

Alright, I've asked three times now and I can hear myself becoming a nuisance, so I'm leaving it. *glances at Orla with a warm half-smile* You know where I am if something comes to mind, love.
