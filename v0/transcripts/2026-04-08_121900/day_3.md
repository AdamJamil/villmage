### The Kettle & Crow — Morning
*Pell Arenway, Sable Dunmore, Ham Birch, Orla Fenn*

**Aldric Moss** `[7]`: *adjusts the measuring tape around my neck and glances up from the ledger I've been reviewing*

Morning, Denn. Your accounts for last month are squared — though I noticed you've been ordering more linseed oil than usual. Either you've picked up a lot of new commission work or you're waterproofing something substantial.

**Pell Arenway** `[7]`: *slides into a chair with my satchel already half-open, pulling out a folded note and setting it on the table near Sable*

"Morning, all. Sable, that's from Margot — she wants to know if you've got any of that smoked salt left from last week."

*glances around the table, pausing briefly on Orla with a quick nod, then turning to Ham*

"How's Duchess? Still terrorizing the lavender?"

**Aldric Moss** `[7]`: *looks up from the ledger, tapping my pen twice on the page before setting it down*

Morning, Denn. Your accounts for last month are squared — though I noticed you've been ordering more linseed oil than usual. Either you've picked up a lot of new commission work or you're waterproofing something substantial.

**Sable Dunmore** `[8]`: *takes the note from Pell, unfolds it with one hand while wiping the other on my apron*

"Tell Margot I've got half a jar left and she can have it Thursday when she comes for the honey tasting — no point making two trips."

*tucks the note into my apron pocket and glances at the table, already clocking who hasn't ordered*

"Ham, you eating or just sitting there looking responsible?"

**Aldric Moss** `[7]`: *realizes I've said the same thing twice and clears my throat, straightening the ledger slightly*

Apologies — morning's getting ahead of me. But the question stands, Denn. The linseed oil.

**Sable Dunmore** `[7]`: *leans against the counter, refilling my own mug first because I've earned it*

"Pell, I'll tell her myself Thursday — she's coming early for the honey tasting and she'll want to argue about it in person anyway."

*turns back to Ham with a look that says I already know the answer*

"And speaking of — how's the lavender situation? Duchess leave you anything worth salvaging, or am I switching to thyme for the rest of the season?"

**Aldric Moss** `[7]`: *taps the ledger entry with one finger, waiting*

The numbers don't lie, Denn. You're up forty percent on linseed oil over the same quarter last year. I'm not prying — I'm reconciling. There's a difference.

**Aldric Moss** `[7]`: *taps the ledger entry one more time, then folds my hands on the counter*

Well, Denn. I've asked three different ways now. I'm starting to think you're either not hearing me or you're hoping I'll stop asking.

**Sable Dunmore** `[7]`: *sets my mug down and looks at Ham, waiting*

"That's not a joke question, by the way — I need to know what I'm working with for Thursday. Margot's coming early and she's already got opinions about the cake, and if I'm switching herbs I'd rather know now than when she's standing in my kitchen smelling things."

**Aldric Moss** `[8]`: *exhales slowly through my nose and straightens the ledger, squaring its edges with the counter*

Right. Well. When you're ready to have a conversation, Denn, I'll be here. I'm not going anywhere — obviously.

*pulls the measuring tape off my neck and begins recoiling it, a nervous habit disguised as tidying*

**Ham Birch** `[7]`: *shifts in my chair, takes a slow sip from my mug before setting it down*

"Duchess got into the starts, not the mature plants. Lavender's fine — mostly. I'll have enough for Thursday if I cut tomorrow morning."

*pauses, looks at Sable steadily*

"Thyme's good either way, though. Wouldn't hurt to have both on hand."

**Aldric Moss** `[7]`: *stops recoiling the measuring tape and sets it on the counter, then looks directly at Denn*

I'm going to take your silence as confirmation that everything's in order and you'd rather not discuss the linseed oil. That's your prerogative. But if someone else starts selling finished oil treatments and undercuts you, don't come to me wondering how they spotted the opportunity before you locked it down.

**Sable Dunmore** `[7]`: *nods, satisfied, and pulls a small notebook from my apron pocket to scratch something down*

"Both. I'll do both. Lavender in the cake, thyme in the crust for the savory plate — Margot can fight me about it Thursday."

*looks up from the notebook at Orla, who hasn't said a word yet*

"Orla, you eating or just keeping the chair warm?"

**Aldric Moss** `[7]`: *slides the ledger to the side and begins straightening a row of candle tins that don't need straightening*

Well. Seven attempts at conversation about linseed oil and I've talked myself into a full monologue. That's a new record, even for me.

*glances at Denn, then away*

You need anything while you're here, or did you just come in to watch me unravel?

**Aldric Moss** `[7]`: *stops fidgeting with the candle tins and just stands there for a moment, letting the silence settle*

Look, Denn. I realize I've been talking at you for five minutes straight about linseed oil like it's a matter of village security. It's not. Your books are clean, your credit's good.

*pauses, then quieter*

How are you?

**Sable Dunmore** `[7]`: *looks up from my notebook at Orla, waiting with the kind of patience I've perfected over ten years of running this place*

"I've got eggs, I've got porridge, and I've got whatever Ham didn't let Duchess get to. Pick one before I pick for you."

**Aldric Moss** `[7]`: *stands behind the counter, letting that last question hang in the air, genuinely meaning it but already regretting how vulnerable it sounded*

...Forget I said that. The linseed oil's accounted for. You need anything from the back?

**Sable Dunmore** `[7]`: *watches Orla for a beat, then sets my own mug down and slides the porridge pot closer to the edge of the counter*

"Eggs it is, then."

*cracks two into the pan without waiting for confirmation, because I know that silence*

"Pell — you and Margot get to the Fenwicks' gate this morning yet, or is that still happening?"

**Denn Corvale** `[6]`: *stands there a moment longer, letting Aldric's embarrassment settle before speaking*

The linseed oil's for a project. Nothing you need to reconcile.

*shifts weight, glances toward the door, then back*

I'm fine, Aldric. You got any of those number-four files in the back? I burned through my last one.

**Pell Arenway** `[7]`: *sets down my mug and shakes my head*

"Haven't been out that way yet — I was going to swing by the Fenwicks' after breakfast. Margot wanted to see the knot on that soap bundle before anyone touches it, so I'm just playing escort at this point."

*glances at Orla, then back to Sable*

"You need me to bring anything back from that direction while I'm out there?"

**Aldric Moss** `[7]`: *feels a small wave of relief wash through me, though I keep my expression neutral*

Number-four files. Yes, I've got a few in the back.

*turns toward the stockroom, then pauses*

And Denn — "a project" is fine. I don't need the details. I just need the numbers to make sense, and they do.

*disappears into the back to fetch the files*

**Aldric Moss** `[3]`: *returns from the back room with two number-four files, sets them on the counter*

Got two left. I'll order more with the next shipment from Bretten — takes about a week.

*slides them toward Denn, then pulls the ledger back and makes a small notation*

**Sable Dunmore** `[7]`: *flips the eggs without looking, already thinking*

"If you're passing the Fenwicks' anyway — see if there's a new bundle. Don't touch it, just look. Margot'll want to know if whoever's making the rounds came back."

*slides Orla's eggs onto a plate with a piece of bread and sets it down in front of her, easy and unceremonious*

**Denn Corvale** `[3]`: *picks up the two files, turns them over in my hands, checking the cut*

These'll do. Put them on my account.

*tucks the files into my apron pocket, then pauses at the counter instead of leaving*

Wren Calloway's supposed to be at the forge this morning. I should get back.

**Pell Arenway** `[7]`: *nods at Sable, filing that away*

"Just look, don't touch — got it. Margot's going to want first crack at that knot anyway."

*takes a bite of bread and glances at Orla's plate, then back to my own mug, keeping my voice easy*

"Orla, you still coming out to Ham's Tuesday for that fence post?"

**Aldric Moss** `[4]`: *notes the purchase in the ledger, then looks up*

Tell Wren I said hello. And remind her we're overdue on the monthly accounts — she'll know what I mean.

*pauses, then adds almost as an afterthought*

The files are on your account. Safe walk back, Denn.

**Orla Fenn** `[6]`: *looks down at the eggs and bread Sable set in front of me, picks up the fork*

"Tuesday still works."

*takes a bite, chews, then adds without looking up*

"I'll bring a spare post in case yours is rotted through at the base. Usually is."

**Denn Corvale** `[3]`: *nods once at Aldric*

I'll tell her. Week's fine on the files.

*turns toward the door, then stops with one hand on the frame*

Aldric. The linseed oil — it's not a business thing. It's personal. Leave it at that.

*steps out into the morning, heading back toward the forge*

*Denn Corvale left.*

**Sable Dunmore** `[4]`: *watches Orla eat without making a thing of it, turns back to Pell*

"Good. And Orla — that's smart about the spare post. Ham's place eats wood like Duchess eats lavender."

*picks up my own mug again, leans against the counter*

"So what time are you and Margot meeting at the gate? I want to know when to expect the full report."

### Fenwick Meadow — Afternoon
*Ham Birch, Orla Fenn, Pell Arenway, Denn Corvale*

**Pell Arenway** `[7]`: *drops satchel on the grass with a thud and stretches*

So I swung by the Fenwicks' gate with Margot this morning — the soap bundle's still there, untouched. Whoever's leaving these isn't coming back to check on them. Ham, did Sable's question from this morning ever land? She was asking if anything besides Duchess had been at your lavender.

**Sable Dunmore** `[8]`: *sets down a plate of bread and honey butter in the middle of the table without being asked*

"Margot, Wren — good timing, both of you. Pell was supposed to be back by now with news from the Fenwicks' gate, but I suppose that's Pell for you."

*glances at the door, then back*

"Either of you seen him on the way in?"

**Ham Birch** `[8]`: *tilts hat back and scratches the side of his jaw*

Haven't checked properly yet — cutting tomorrow morning, so I'll look close then. But I'll say this: Duchess doesn't trample things she doesn't have a reason to trample. If something else was in there first, she'd have made a mess of it on principle.

**Sable Dunmore** `[8]`: *wipes hands on apron and slides into the seat across from them, pulling a piece of bread toward herself*

"He said midday. It's past midday. Which means either he found something worth looking at or he got distracted by a bird — and with Pell, honestly, the odds are even."

*looks at Margot*

"You were there this morning too, weren't you? At the gate? Did you two not walk back together?"

**Margot Thistle** `[8]`: *tears off a piece of bread, dips it in the honey butter*

"We walked to the gate together, but I left him there. He wanted to check the post on the south side — said the knot had been retied. I wasn't going to stand around waiting while he narrated his findings to himself, so I came back to open the shop."

*chews, then points the bread at Sable*

"If he's not here by the time I finish this bread, he's fine. He's just being Pell."

**Pell Arenway** `[7]`: *picks a stray grass blade off the satchel flap and flicks it away*

That's what I figured. If something had been rooting around in there before her, she'd have gone through it like a personal grievance. So either it was just Duchess being Duchess, or whatever was there didn't leave enough scent to bother her — which narrows things down a bit.

*glances at Orla, then back to Ham*

Orla, you're still good for Tuesday to look at those stems?

**Orla Fenn** `[7]`: *nods once*

Tuesday's good. I'm bringing a spare post anyway — if the base is rotted out we won't want to waste the trip.

**Sable Dunmore** `[7]`: *takes a bite of bread, chews slowly, points at Margot with the same casual authority Margot just used*

"The knot had been *retied*? As in someone undid it and put it back, or retied differently?"

*leans back, brushes a flour smudge off her sleeve*

"Because those are two very different stories."

**Pell Arenway** `[7]`: *nods at Orla, then turns to Denn, who's been standing there quiet as a fencepost*

Denn, you've been awful still for a man who usually has an opinion about everything. Something on your mind, or are you just enjoying the sunshine?

**Sable Dunmore** `[9]`: *nods slowly, sets down the bread*

"That's exactly the right question. Because if it's retied *differently*, that means someone else has been at that gate since Pell first spotted it."

*taps the table once with her finger*

"Margot, was it the same double-loop he described, or something else?"

**Denn Corvale** `[6]`: *shifts weight slightly, squints out across the meadow*

Enjoying the sunshine, mostly.

*pauses, then after a beat*

Wren Calloway came by the forge this morning for that history of hers. Asked good questions. She wanted to know about every lock I've changed in forty years — I told her more than I meant to.

**Wren Calloway** `[8]`: *looks up from the leather notebook, pen hovering*

"I haven't seen Pell since yesterday. But Margot — the knot. Was it the same one or wasn't it? Because if someone's been at the Fenwick gate, I want to know about it. That meadow's been too quiet lately."

**Pell Arenway** `[8]`: *raises eyebrows at Denn*

Every lock in forty years — that's generous of you. What's Wren building, a history or a confession booth?

*grins, but there's genuine curiosity underneath it*

She gave me a cartography book once after making me cry about a split infinitive. I'd walk through fire for that woman, but I'd proofread the note first.

**Margot Thistle** `[8]`: *sets down the bread, brushes crumbs off fingers*

"It wasn't the same knot. The double-loop was gone — someone retied it as a plain square knot. Tighter, too, like they were in a hurry or didn't know the original."

*looks at Sable*

"Which means either Pell retied it himself to test something — which, knowing him, is possible — or someone else has been back to that gate since last night."

**Pell Arenway** `[7]`: *laughs, then lets it settle*

Anyway — Margot and I checked the Fenwick gate bundle this morning like we said we would. Still wrapped tight, sprig intact, no sign anyone's been back for it. Whatever this is, it's not a sales pitch. You don't leave product on a fence and walk away unless you're giving something, not selling it.

**Sable Dunmore** `[9]`: *drums fingers on the table once, then stops*

"So someone's been back to that gate. And they didn't know — or didn't care — how the original was tied."

*looks toward the door again, then back at Margot*

"That settles it. Pell didn't retie his own knot wrong. He'd have told us about it in detail whether we wanted him to or not."

**Denn Corvale** `[4]`: *nods slowly, eyes still on the meadow*

Giving something. That's what I told Wren this morning — you can tell a lot about a person by what they leave behind and whether they come back for it.

*pauses, then quieter*

Most don't.

**Sable Dunmore** `[9]`: *pulls the bread plate closer, tears off another piece but doesn't eat it yet*

"So we've got someone making a circuit, leaving soap bundles tied with a knot that says 'open me,' and now someone *else* — or the same someone in a hurry — has been back to retie the gate with the wrong knot."

*looks at Wren*

"You said the meadow's been too quiet. How quiet are we talking?"

**Pell Arenway** `[7]`: *lets Denn's words sit for a moment, then nods slowly*

Most don't. That's true enough.

*adjusts the satchel strap on his shoulder, glances toward the Fenwick gate road*

Well, whoever this is — they left something, they didn't come back, and they tied it so it'd open clean. That's not careless. That's deliberate kindness or deliberate strangeness, and I haven't figured out which yet.

**Sable Dunmore** `[8]`: *leans forward, elbows on the table, watching Wren*

"Because if the meadow's been empty *and* someone's making trips to that gate after dark, those two things might be the same thing. Quiet doesn't always mean nothing's happening — sometimes it means someone's waiting for everyone else to stop looking."

**Denn Corvale** `[4]`: *quiet for a long moment, then speaks without looking away from the meadow*

Deliberate kindness and deliberate strangeness come from the same place, most of the time. Someone who ties a clean hitch knot on a gift they don't stay to watch opened — that's someone who's practiced at giving things up.

**Wren Calloway** `[8]`: *sets down the pen, looks at Sable over the glasses*

"Three mornings now I've sat in an empty schoolroom. No children, no parents stopping by, nothing. The meadow's the same — Margot mentioned it was empty on day one, and it hasn't changed since."

*pauses, taps the notebook*

"So yes, quiet. The kind of quiet where you start noticing what's missing instead of what's there."

**Pell Arenway** `[6]`: *lets Denn's words settle, then exhales slowly through his nose*

That's a hell of a thing to say on a Tuesday afternoon, Denn.

*quieter, adjusting the satchel strap*

But you're not wrong. Whoever this is — they're not waiting around for a thank you. That's either the best kind of person or the loneliest.

**Sable Dunmore** `[8]`: *sets down the bread, lets the silence sit for exactly one beat*

"Three mornings. No children, no foot traffic in the meadow, and someone's making trips to the Fenwick gate after dark."

*looks at Margot, then Wren, gold tooth catching the light as she presses her lips together*

"That's not three separate things. That's one thing we haven't named yet."

**Pell Arenway** `[4]`: *quiet for a moment, then shifts the satchel to his other shoulder*

Could be both, honestly. Wouldn't be the first time.

*glances at Orla*

You still thinking about that hitch knot?

**Wren Calloway** `[8]`: *closes the notebook slowly, leaves one finger marking the page*

"No, it isn't three separate things."

*looks at Sable, then at Margot, voice quieter than before*

"But I'd rather not name it wrong. Margot — when you saw the square knot this morning, was the gate itself still latched? Or just tied shut?"

**Orla Fenn** `[4]`: *meets Pell's glance, then looks back out at the meadow*

I've been thinking about it. Whoever tied that knot works with their hands regular. That's not something you learn from a book.

**Margot Thistle** `[9]`: *pauses, thinks back carefully*

"The gate was just tied. No latch — the latch pin's been missing for weeks, maybe longer. That's why someone bothered with the knot in the first place."

*looks at Wren, then Sable*

"Which means whoever retied it knew the latch was broken. That's not a stranger."

**Sable Dunmore** `[9]`: *lets that land, nods once*

"Not a stranger. So it's someone who knows the gate, knows the latch has been gone, and either doesn't know Pell's knots or doesn't care about matching them."

*looks at Margot*

"How many people in this village even use that gate regularly?"

**Pell Arenway** `[4]`: *nods at Orla, then looks down at his hands like he's seeing them for the first time*

That's what I keep coming back to. The knot, the clean wrap, the lavender sprig placed just so — this isn't someone's first bundle. They've done this before, maybe not here, but somewhere.

*quiet for a beat, then looks at Denn*

Denn, in forty years of locks — you ever have someone leave something *at* a door instead of asking you to open one?

**Pell Arenway** `[7]`: *watches Denn's face carefully, genuinely curious*

That's not a small question, I know. Take your time with it.

**Margot Thistle** `[8]`: *counts on fingers, frowning*

"The Fenwicks themselves, obviously. Pell. Me, as of this morning. Ham cuts through there sometimes when he's checking the south beds. Maybe Denn, if he's taking the shortcut to the creek."

*pauses, looks at Sable*

"That's five people, and I'd bet my shop none of them tie a square knot in a hurry."

**Sable Dunmore** `[8]`: *sets down the bread, leans back, crosses her arms*

"Five people. And none of them tie a square knot."

*looks at the door one more time, then back at the table*
