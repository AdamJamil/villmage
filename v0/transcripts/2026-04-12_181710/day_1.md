### The Calloway Academy — Morning
*Wren Calloway, Orla Fenn*

**Wren Calloway** `[7]`: (You eye Orla over the top of your glasses, making a mental note in your worn leather notebook. She's early today. Good. Punctuality is a virtue.)

"Morning, Orla. You're here bright and early. Something interesting for the records, perhaps?"

**Pell Arenway** `[9]`: My satchel jingles with what sounds suspiciously like a stray acorn as I make my way to the counter. "Morning, everyone! Sable, is that... yes! The smell of fresh bread is already making this day feel achievable. Any early deliveries today, or is everyone just enjoying the calm before the rush?"

**Wren Calloway** `[9]`: "Just going over the attendance for the spring term, Orla. Making sure everyone's accounted for, even the stragglers. You wouldn't believe the excuses I get for tardiness these days." You tap your pen against the open page of your notebook, a faint, rhythmic sound.

**Sable Dunmore** `[8]`: I chuckle, wiping flour off my hands onto my apron. "Morning, Pell! Achievable is right, the way you say it, I might even believe it. No early deliveries, just getting the 'Pell's Precarious Porridge' ready for the lunch crowd. And yes, fresh bread. The scent of it alone pays the rent." I nod towards Margot. "Margot, dear, don't tell me you're up before the sun today just for a whiff of my sourdough?"

**Wren Calloway** `[7]`: "Indeed. Though I find a well-crafted lie almost as interesting as the truth, Orla. Keeps the mind sharp, doesn't it?" You raise an eyebrow, a hint of a challenge in your tone. "Speaking of which, anything new from the... capital, perhaps? I hear whispers of changes, as always."

**Sable Dunmore** `[8]`: I grin, turning to Margot and leaning against the counter, still wiping my hands with the apron. "Well, someone has to keep an eye on what you're up to, dear. Wouldn't want you to miss out on the early morning gossip, would we?" I wink, then glance back at Pell. "And as for achieved, Pell, let's see how precariously you balance that porridge when it's full of Ham's surprisingly lumpy oats."

**Wren Calloway** `[7]`: "The capital always has whispers, Orla. Rarely do they amount to anything more than a slight adjustment to the price of ink, but one must always be prepared." You tap your pen against your notebook again, then look at her expectantly. "So, no news then? Nothing of substance?"

**Margot Thistle** `[9]`: I lean against the counter, already nursing a mug of something suspiciously dark and likely bitter. "Morning, everyone," I respond, my voice a little rough. "Sable, your sourdough is certainly one motivator. And the other is the persistent cough I developed from Ham's 'surprisingly lumpy' oats." I take a slow sip of my drink, then raise an eyebrow at Sable. "Though, if gossip is what you're after, I did see Orla's lights on before dawn this morning. Something big must be brewing over at the forge."

**Wren Calloway** `[8]`: "Pity. The fewer surprises from the capital, the better, I always say. Though I suppose a dull day is a productive one for my attendance records." You sigh, closing your notebook with a decisive thud. "Now, about the overdue books... have you seen the copy of 'The Practical Farmer' anywhere, Orla? It's been out for weeks."

**Margot Thistle** `[8]`: "Orla," I muse, taking another slow sip, the bitter liquid doing little to soothe my throat. "Well, that's interesting. I was under the impression her forge was quiet until mid-morning." I glance between Sable and Pell. "Perhaps she's finally getting around to making those custom drying racks I commissioned."

**Wren Calloway** `[8]`: "It was last signed out by that young Mr. Fenwick, I believe. The one with the perpetually smudged nose and an alarming lack of understanding regarding crop rotation. He insists he returned it, but my shelves tell a different story." You eye Orla pointedly. "Perhaps you've seen him about?"

**Pell Arenway** `[9]`: I jingle a bit as I shift my satchel, a stray acorn rolling perilously close to the edge of the counter before I scoop it up. "Oh, Orla's been up for a while now, Margot. I actually passed her just as I was heading this way – looked like she was wrestling a new shipment of raw iron off a cart. Whatever she's making, it's going to be heavy." I tuck the acorn back into my satchel with a slight grin. "So maybe it *is* those drying racks, but I bet they'll be able to hold a whole forest."

**Wren Calloway** `[7]`: "Young Fenwick," you repeat, a slight frown creasing your brow. "Always with his head in the clouds, that one. If you happen upon him, Orla, do remind him that 'The Practical Farmer' is not merely a suggestion, but a vital guide for those who wish to avoid… well, a lack of practical farming." You cross your arms, a silent challenge in your gaze. "Unless, of course, you've decided to keep an eye on him yourself?"

**Pell Arenway** `[8]`: I tap my chin thoughtfully, a faint jingle from my satchel accompanying the gesture. "A whole forest, maybe! Though I wouldn't put it past Orla to build something that could hold a small mountain range if she put her mind to it. She's got that kind of determination, you know?"

**Wren Calloway** `[8]`: "Just trying to ensure our future farmers aren't… well, *un*farmed, Orla. A simple task, you'd think." You tap your foot impatiently. "So, about Mr. Fenwick, then? Any thoughts on his whereabouts, or perhaps his general aptitude for agriculture?"

**Sable Dunmore** `[8]`: I lean back against the counter, still with my apron in hand, though I've stopped wiping and just let it hang. "A whole forest, maybe a mountain range," I muse, a small smile playing on my lips. "Well, that's Orla for you. I just hope she remembers those drying racks need to be sturdy enough for laundry, not just lumber." I look between Pell and Margot, my eyes twinkling. "Unless you two have some exceptionally heavy socks you're planning on washing."

**Margot Thistle** `[9]`: I finish my sip, the bitter tea finally making a dent in my persistent cough. "Well, if Orla is building drying racks that can hold a forest, I'm sure my 'exceptionally heavy socks' will be perfectly fine." I pause, a mischievous glint in my eye. "Though I wouldn't mind a custom-designed shelf for my new collection of obscure fungi."

**Wren Calloway** `[8]`: "I suppose that's one way to look at it," you say, tapping your foot again. "Though I find a thorough understanding of the basics tends to prevent a good deal of future 'excitement.' Now, about the book. Did you happen to see him heading towards the old mill, perhaps? He always seems to gravitate towards places with more gears than sense."

**Sable Dunmore** `[9]`: I tap my fingers on the counter, a faint smile on my lips as Margot mentions her fungi. "Well, if anyone can make a fungi shelf that doubles as a defensive barrier against stray acorns, it's Orla. Just make sure she knows they're for *display* and not for, oh, building a new roof." I wink at Pell.

**Wren Calloway** `[7]`: "The mill, you say? Well, that's not exactly the most fertile ground for a farmer, is it? Though I suppose it's a fitting metaphor for his current state of agricultural knowledge." You sigh, tapping your pen against the notebook. "Perhaps he's trying to grind his brain into something useful."

**Sable Dunmore** `[8]`: "Oh, I'm sure she will," I say, a knowing twinkle in my eye as I consider Margot's fungi. "Orla's got a knack for making things just a *touch* more robust than strictly necessary. Just don't let her add any fancy, heavy-duty hinges to your mushroom display, or you'll need a forklift to open it." I glance at Pell, a small smile playing on my lips. "Though I suppose if anyone has a forklift handy, it'd be Pell."

**Wren Calloway** `[8]`: "Well, if he's not at the mill, perhaps he's decided to embark on a new career path altogether. 'The Practical Miller,' perhaps? Though I doubt he'd fare much better there." You tap your pen against your notebook, a thoughtful expression on your face. "Any other bright ideas, Orla? Or shall I simply dispatch a search party?"

**Pell Arenway** `[8]`: I chuckle, the faint jingle of my satchel echoing my amusement. "A forklift might be a bit much for fungi, Sable, even for Orla! Though if anyone could rig one up out of spare parts and sheer willpower, it'd be her." I glance at Margot, a thoughtful expression on my face. "But a robust display is a good idea. Wouldn't want those rare fungi getting knocked about, especially if they're, uh, *reactive*."

**Wren Calloway** `[8]`: "A search party for a missing book? Don't be ridiculous, Orla. I simply wish to know if he has developed a sudden interest in… something other than what he *should* be doing. Perhaps he's decided to take up poetry instead of proper crop rotation." I tap my pen against my notebook, a new, slightly irritated rhythm.

**Pell Arenway** `[7]`: I nod, my satchel jangling softly with the movement. "Reactive fungi, that's a new one for my delivery list! I'll make sure to mention the *display only* part to Orla, just in case she gets any ideas about turning them into, oh, say, self-stirring porridge." I grin at Sable. "Though, a self-stirring porridge *would* save you some arm work, wouldn't it?"

**Wren Calloway** `[8]`: "Poetry? A farmer? Now *that* would be something for the records." You tap your pen again, a new thought dawning. "Unless, of course, he's discovered a sudden passion for… well, something that actually involves dirt. Perhaps he's finally taken 'The Practical Farmer' to heart and is out in the fields, attempting to commune with the soil." You raise an eyebrow, a hint of skepticism in your gaze. "Though I wouldn't put it past him to be reciting sonnets to the scarecrows."

**Sable Dunmore** `[8]`: I laugh, a warm, genuine sound that crinkles the laugh lines around my eyes. "Self-stirring porridge, Pell? Now you're just talking crazy! Though, I suppose it'd be one less thing for me to argue with Ham about. I can just hear it now: 'Ham, your oats are too lumpy, the self-stirring fungi are protesting!'" I wink, then lean back against the counter, crossing my arms. "But speaking of things that save arm work, Margot, how's that cough of yours doing with Orla's pre-dawn hammering?"

**Wren Calloway** `[7]`: "Well, if he's communing with the soil, I only hope he's using the appropriate terminology. Wouldn't want him confusing a perennial with a periwinkle." You tap your pen against your chin, a glint in your eye. "Though, if he's truly taken to poetry, perhaps he could compose an ode to the overdue book. It might be less work than actually finding it."

**Sable Dunmore** `[8]`: I shrug, a little smile playing on my lips. "Oh, the hammering's a soothing lullaby compared to Ham's early morning grumbling. Anyway, you look like you could use a refill, Margot. Another round of... 'Ham's Harsh Brew'?"

**Wren Calloway** `[7]`: "An ode, yes. Perhaps he could call it 'The Ballad of the Borrower Who Forgot.' Though I doubt it would be as effective as simply returning the book." You sigh, pushing your glasses further up your nose. "Unless, of course, you've decided to track him down yourself, Orla? You seem to have a knack for finding the unexpected."

**Margot Thistle** `[7]`: I shake my head, a dry chuckle escaping me. "Ham's Harsh Brew, indeed. No thanks, Sable, I've had enough of his 'natural remedies' for one morning." I push my now-empty mug forward. "Just plain hot water, please. I'll add my own."

**Wren Calloway** `[7]`: "Precisely. So, unless you've suddenly developed a burning desire to become a detective of lost literature, I suppose I'll have to resign myself to the fact that 'The Practical Farmer' is off on an adventure of its own." You tap your pen against your notebook, a sign that you're ready to move on. "Anything else noteworthy before the morning truly gets underway, Orla? I have lessons to prepare, and a rather unenthusiastic group of pupils to enlighten."

**Sable Dunmore** `[8]`: "Plain hot water, coming right up," I say, already reaching for the kettle. "Though I'll warn you, even the water around here has a bit of Ham's grumpiness infused in it if you listen close enough."

**Wren Calloway** `[7]`: "Good. Then perhaps I can finally get these attendance sheets sorted without further distraction." I nod towards my notebook, then glance at the door. "Unless, of course, *you* have a lesson to prepare, Orla?"

**Sable Dunmore** `[9]`: I pour the hot water, a little steam rising around my face. "Don't tempt me, Margot. Ham's got enough grump for an entire village. And as for listening close, I hear him sometimes, grumbling to his prize-winning pumpkins about my 'impertinent prices.'" I place the mug in front of her.

**Orla Fenn** `[5]`: "No," you say, your voice flat. You turn to leave, already planning the cut list for a new table. "Just checking in."

**Sable Dunmore** `[9]`: "Oh, he grumbles about everything, bless his heart," I add, shaking my head with a smile. "He once complained my bread was too fluffy. Too fluffy! Can you imagine?"

*Orla Fenn left.*
